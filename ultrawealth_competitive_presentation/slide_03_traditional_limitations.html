Traditional Robo-Advisors: Three Core Limitations

Despite strong recent performance, all Australian robo-advisors share critical architectural weaknesses
► 01. STATIC ALGORITHMS
Modern Portfolio Theory with manual recalibration. Algorithms cannot adapt to changing market conditions without human intervention. Fixed rules lag market regime changes during volatility.
► 02. TEMPLATE PORTFOLIOS
3-7 predefined portfolios based on risk questionnaires. All investors with similar risk scores receive identical allocations regardless of unique circumstances, goals, or behavioral patterns.
► 03. PERIODIC REBALANCING
Scheduled rebalancing (daily/weekly/monthly) rather than opportunistic. Cannot capitalize on intraday market movements or respond immediately to significant events. Transaction timing is arbitrary.
